# HR & Employee Services

The HR module covers day-to-day people operations: HR cases, leave, attendance,
expense claims, the holiday calendar, OKRs, and employee records. Employees interact
through the **Employee Portal**; HR staff work from **People & Workplace → HR**.

## Employee Portal (self-service)

The portal (`/portal`) is the front door for every employee. From the home screen
they can:

- Submit a new request (HR case, IT ticket, equipment, etc.)
- Track the status of their existing requests
- Search the Knowledge Base
- View assets assigned to them

The welcome banner shows a count of the employee's open and resolved requests.

## HR cases

HR cases handle structured people processes such as **onboarding** and
**offboarding**. A case is made up of templated tasks assigned to the responsible
teams (IT, Finance, Facilities, etc.), and each task is checked off as it completes.

Typical onboarding tasks: set up email, assign a laptop, grant building access,
assign a buddy. Typical offboarding tasks: return badge, deactivate accounts,
knowledge transfer, final clearance.

## Leave requests

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Submitted, awaiting manager approval. |
| **Approved** | Manager approved; leave balance deducted, calendar blocked. |
| **Rejected** | Manager declined. |
| **Cancelled** | Withdrawn by the employee or manager after approval. |

**Flow:** the employee submits a request (start date, end date, reason, number of
days); the manager reviews it — seeing the employee's current balance — and approves
or rejects with an optional comment. Leave types (sick, casual, PTO, unpaid, etc.)
and accrual rules are configurable.

## Attendance

Daily attendance is recorded as present, absent, half-day, on-leave, or
work-from-home, and aggregated to a monthly attendance percentage. Absences drive
**loss of pay (LOP)**, which reduces gross salary in the next payroll run.

## Expense claims

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Employee is still editing. |
| **Submitted** | Locked and sent for review. |
| **Under review** | Manager / Finance reviewing. |
| **Approved** | Amount locked. |
| **Rejected** | Declined with a reason. |
| **Reimbursed → Paid** | Payment processed and cleared. |
| **Cancelled** | Cancelled by the employee or admin. |

**Flow:** the employee creates a report and adds line items (category, amount,
receipt, date, merchant), then submits. The manager reviews against policy, Finance
calculates the reimbursable amount, and once approved Finance generates the payment.
Expense categories include travel, accommodation, meals, transport, office supplies,
software, training, and medical. Policy limits (e.g. a daily meal cap, a receipt
requirement above a threshold) are enforced during review.

## Holiday calendar & OKRs

- **Holiday calendar** (`/app/holidays`) — the org-wide list of public and optional
  holidays, used by the leave and SLA business calendars.
- **OKRs & Goals** (`/app/okr`) — objectives and key results for individuals and
  teams, also surfaced inside Performance.

## Employee records

Each employee record carries name, employee ID, department, title, manager,
employment type, location, start date, and the assigned salary structure. Employment
status moves through **Active**, **Probation**, **On leave**, **Resigned**,
**Terminated**, and **Offboarded**.
