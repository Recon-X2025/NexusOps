# Procurement

The Procurement module manages the purchase lifecycle: purchase requests, approvals,
purchase orders, goods receipt, and the three-way match that gates invoice payment. It
lives under **Finance & Procurement → Procurement** (`/app/procurement`).

## Purchase requests

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Being created; still editable. |
| **Pending** | Awaiting approval. |
| **Approved** | Passed the approval gate(s). |
| **Rejected** | Approval denied. |
| **Ordered** | A purchase order was raised from the request. |
| **Received** | Goods received (a GRN exists). |
| **Cancelled** | Cancelled by the requester. |

A request has line items (description, quantity, unit price, optional vendor, optional
asset type). The total is the sum of quantity × unit price across all lines. The
approval route is decided when the request is created and cannot change afterwards.

### Approval tiers

Approval routing is configurable per organisation. A typical setup:

- **Auto-approve** below a configured threshold (e.g. ₹25,000)
- **Department head** for the mid band (e.g. ₹25,000 – ₹500,000)
- **VP Finance** above the upper threshold (e.g. ₹500,000+)

## Purchase orders

A purchase order (PO) is raised from an approved request. It records the vendor, the
**legal entity** (for tax and invoicing), delivery date, total amount, notes, and line
items. Tagging the correct legal entity matters: if an invoice is later tagged to a
different entity, the three-way match flags a mismatch.

## Three-way match

Before an invoice is paid, CoheronConnect checks that the **invoice**, the **PO**, and
the **goods receipt note (GRN)** agree. The logic lives in
`apps/api/src/lib/invoice-po-match.ts` and runs within an org-configurable tolerance.

The match proceeds in layers:

1. **Header match** — invoice total ≈ PO total, within tolerance.
2. **Line match** — invoice lines are paired to PO lines (by position first, then by
   normalised description, then by a lowest-cost greedy pairing). Each matched line
   must agree within tolerance.
3. **GRN match** — the received value on the GRN ≈ the invoice total, within
   tolerance.

If all three layers pass, the result is **true** and the invoice can be paid. If any
layer fails, the invoice is flagged for manual review, and the result includes the
per-line deltas (invoice minus PO, and the three-way gap) so you can see exactly where
the variance is.
