# GST & TDS (India Tax)

CoheronConnect includes India-specific tax engines for **GST** (Goods and Services
Tax) and **TDS / income tax**. These power invoicing, payroll, and the GST returns in
the Accounting module.

## GST

GST is computed by `computeGST()` in `apps/api/src/lib/india/gst-engine.ts`. The split
depends on whether the supply is intra-state or inter-state:

| Supply | Components | Split |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Intra-state** (same state) | CGST + SGST | 50% / 50% of the total GST |
| **Inter-state** (different state) | IGST | 100% as a single component |

- **Intra-state**: a supply within the same state attracts Central GST (CGST) and
  State GST (SGST) in equal halves. Input tax credit is tracked separately for each.
- **Inter-state**: a supply across state lines attracts a single Integrated GST
  (IGST) — there is no CGST/SGST split.
- **Reverse charge**: when flagged on an invoice, the buyer (not the seller) is liable
  for the GST. This affects how the line appears in GSTR-1 / GSTR-3B.

Typical rates are 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28% depending on the goods or service.

## TDS / income tax

Income tax is computed by `computeTax()` in `apps/api/src/lib/india-tax-engine.ts`.
The engine evaluates **both** the Old and New regimes; the employee's chosen regime
determines the TDS applied in payroll.

### New regime slabs

| Annual income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| 0 – ₹3 L | 0% |
| ₹3 L – ₹6 L | 5% |
| ₹6 L – ₹9 L | 10% |
| ₹9 L – ₹12 L | 15% |
| ₹12 L – ₹15 L | 20% |
| Above ₹15 L | 30% |

The New regime applies a flat standard deduction and does **not** allow itemised
Chapter VI-A deductions.

### Old regime slabs

| Annual income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| 0 – ₹2.5 L | 0% |
| ₹2.5 L – ₹5 L | 5% |
| ₹5 L – ₹10 L | 20% |
| Above ₹10 L | 30% |

The Old regime allows itemised deductions:

- **80C** — up to ₹1.5 L (LIC, PPF, ELSS, tuition fees, etc.)
- **80D** — health insurance premiums
- **80CCD(1B)** — NPS contribution up to ₹50 K (over and above 80C)
- **80TTA** — savings-account interest up to ₹10 K
- **Section 24(b)** — home-loan interest (self-occupied) up to ₹2 L
- **HRA exemption** — the least of: actual HRA, 40% of basic, or rent paid minus 10%
  of basic

Surcharge and health-&-education cess apply to both regimes.

### Monthly TDS in payroll

Payroll spreads the annual liability evenly across the financial year:

```
monthly TDS = total tax liability ÷ months remaining in the financial year
```

For mid-year joiners, the liability is pro-rated across the remaining months. The TDS
computed here is the deduction applied at step 8 of the
[payroll run](/modules/payroll).

## Form 16

At year-end, employees receive **Form 16** — their TDS certificate — sent for
e-signature from the **TDS Declarations** tab in the Payroll module.
