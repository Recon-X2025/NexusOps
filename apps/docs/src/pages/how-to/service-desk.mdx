# How-To: Service Desk & IT

Tasks for IT agents and service-desk teams. Full reference:
[Tickets](/modules/tickets).

## How do I create a ticket?

- **Web:** click **New Ticket**, choose the type (Incident, Service Request, Problem,
  Change), set a title, description, category, and priority, then submit.
- **Email:** send to your configured inbound address — the subject becomes the title
  and the body becomes the description.
- **API:** `POST` to `tickets.create` with a bearer token.

## What do the priorities mean for SLA?

| Priority | Response | Resolution |
|----------|---------:|-----------:|
| Critical | 15 min | 4 hours |
| High | 1 hour | 8 hours |
| Medium | 4 hours | 24 hours |
| Low | 8 hours | 72 hours |

The SLA indicator on each ticket is green (on track), amber (at risk, under 20% time
left), or red (breached).

## Why did my ticket's SLA clock stop?

Because the ticket's status category is **Pending** — for example waiting on a
customer or vendor. SLA clocks pause while a ticket is pending and resume when it
leaves that state.

## How do I find and work the right tickets?

Open **Service Desk** (`/app/tickets`). Switch between **Overview** (KPIs), **Queue**
(table), and **Board** (Kanban). Use search, the status tabs, and filters (type, SLA,
date) to narrow the list. Select multiple tickets to **bulk assign**, **bulk close**,
or **export to CSV**.

## How do I resolve and close a ticket?

Work the ticket and add comments as you go. When you've applied a fix, set the status
to **Resolved** (this stops the resolution SLA). Once the user confirms — or after your
configured auto-close window — set it to **Closed**.

## How do I handle a major incident?

Major incidents (`/app/it-services/major-incidents`) get a **war room**
(`.../war-room/[ticketId]`) for coordinated communications. Comms are recorded against
the incident so there's a single source of truth during the outage.

## How do I link a ticket to a problem or change?

On the ticket's **Related** tab, add a linked ticket by UUID and choose the relation
type (related, duplicate, blocks, blocked_by). Formal problems and changes live in
their own modules and are surfaced here too.

## How do I raise a change?

Go to **Changes** (`/app/changes`), create a change request, assign it, and progress
it through its lifecycle. Changes are the planned-modification ticket type and follow
your change-approval (CAB) process.
